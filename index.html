Elements of a Trade Setup

The foundation of ICT's methodology: Understanding the 4 market conditions and 4 institutional order flow reference points that create high-probability trade setups.

4 Market Conditions

Markets exist in one of four states at any given time:

  • Expansion - Strong directional moves
  • Retracement - Pullbacks within trends
  • Reversal - Trend direction changes
  • Consolidation - Range-bound price action

Institutional Order Flow

Four reference points smart money uses:

  • Order Blocks - Institutional accumulation zones
  • Fair Value Gaps - Imbalance areas
  • Liquidity Pools - Stop clusters
  • Equilibrium - Fair value price

Market Condition + Tool

Each market condition pairs with specific tools:

  • Expansion → Order Blocks
  • Retracement → Fair Value Gaps
  • Reversal → Liquidity Pools
  • Consolidation → Equilibrium

Market Efficiency Paradigm

Understanding the fundamental difference between retail and institutional perspectives on market efficiency. Markets are efficient for smart money, not for retail traders.

Uninformed Money (Retail)

  • Believes indicators drive price
  • Thinks retail sentiment moves markets
  • Chases breakouts and news events
  • Uses stop losses predictably
  • Represents 90% of traders but minimal influence

Smart Money (Institutions)

  • Algorithmic price delivery
  • Creates liquidity voids/gaps
  • Hunts retail stop clusters
  • Accumulates at discount prices
  • Distributes at premium prices

Interbank Price Algorithm

  • 90% of FX is algorithmically traded
  • Based on institutional order flow principles
  • Leaves clear "fingerprints" in price action
  • Exploits predictable human behavior (fear/greed)
  • Creates illusion of randomness

Retail vs. Institutional Mindset

Retail Perspective

  • Indicators drive price
  • News moves markets
  • Breakouts continue
  • Chases price action
  • Uses tight stops

Institutional Perspective

  • Order flow drives price
  • Liquidity hunts move markets
  • Breakouts often reverse
  • Fades extremes
  • Uses liquidity pools

Equilibrium vs Discount

Institutional accumulation zones: How banks buy at discount prices below equilibrium. Identifying optimal trade entry points for long positions.

Equilibrium (50% Fib)

  • Fair value price in current range
  • Neutral institutional interest
  • Consolidation common at this level
  • Not ideal for new positions
  • Midpoint of impulse swing

Discount (62-79% Fib)

  • Below equilibrium value
  • Institutional accumulation zone
  • Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) for longs
  • Often paired with bullish order blocks
  • Price doesn't stay long at discount

Turtle Soup Buys

  • False breakdown below key support
  • Hunts sell-stop liquidity
  • Aggressive reversal from discount
  • High probability long setup
  • Confirms with bullish price action

Equilibrium vs Premium

Institutional distribution zones: How banks sell at premium prices above equilibrium. Identifying optimal short entry points.

Equilibrium (50% Fib)

  • Fair value in current range
  • Transition zone between discount/premium
  • Often consolidates at this level
  • No institutional edge
  • Midpoint of price swing

Premium (38-50% Fib)

  • Above equilibrium value
  • Institutional distribution zone
  • Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) for shorts
  • Often paired with bearish order blocks
  • Retail chases price here

Turtle Soup Shorts

  • False breakout above key resistance
  • Hunts buy-stop liquidity
  • Aggressive reversal from premium
  • High probability short setup
  • Confirms with bearish price action
